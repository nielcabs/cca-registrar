INTRODUCTION
Background of the Study
The Registrar's Office is said to be one of the most important offices in schools for handling important records, schedule of the students. This is an office where the records are stored, requests for other academic needs are submitted, and the schedule of admission of students, and other transactions in the school are made. The registrar’s office is responsible for handling the submitted documents during enrollment, transcripts, certificates and managing the students data and requests (Mallares et al., 2024). Management of this office is imperative as service to students, faculty and administration is required and needs timely response. 


In most institutions most of their tasks are done manually, leading to mismanagement of credentials, delay of transactions, and data loss or damage. The office urgently needed not only manpower, but also a digital system to enhance efficiency of the office transactions (“Digital Archives: School and Academic Records for Preservation and Accessibility,” 2025). Requisition and scheduling of registrar services is done manually, or by procedures that are not unified. Document requisition such as transcript requests, certificates and other academic documents are all done on paper forms or electronic communication platforms which are not well integrated. Scheduling for registrar services, on the other hand, may involve walk-ins, appointment diaries or emails. Handling these tasks manually is both time-consuming and risky, especially since it involves processing sensitive student information. Moreover, students and clients often complain about delays and missing documents, unaware of the complexities involved in record management (“Digital Archives: School and Academic Records for Preservation and Accessibility,” 2025). This methodology is characterized by long queues, misplaced requisition requests, discrepancies in documentation and several challenges in checking status of requests made.


The absence of an integrated system can also modulate the efficiency of the registrar office organization. This rework not only reduces efficiency, but also consumes time and resources that could be dedicated to strategic initiatives (Agal et al., 2025). Scarcity conditions cannot smooth the process for the employees to screen the demands, dilute the preferences and hasten the time schedule due to absence of requisition and standard time agenda. The situation can lead to misunderstanding and prolongation of the receipt of the documents, absence of scheduling procedure and transparency for the students to be informed on the status of their request.


In the City College of Angeles Registrar's Office, students are required to clear checks from the different offices with a view of fulfilling all academic and institutional requirements. However it is not always easy to apply the clearance process, especially when the records are stored in a different directory or the manual process. 


Based on the City College of Angeles student handbook 6th edition published in 2022, the student may need to first pay the transcript fee  or any necessary fee to the budget and finance office before they can proceed to clearance signing. Each department has different rules and policies that they follow, they are responsible to identify students obligations, requirements, and qualifications before signing clearance. For instance, the library may deny clearance to any student if he/she borrowed the books that haven't been returned yet. Similarly, a student's clearance may also be denied by the finance office if he/she has outstanding payment of tuition and other fees. 


Other departments that are also involved in the verification process. According to the personnel interviewed from the Community Extension Office, which handles NSTP or National Service Training Program, they require students to accomplish tree planting and community extension projects that are assigned by their respective professor, and may also be denied from request if not met. The Multimedia and Information Systems and Service Office (MISSO) may also deny clearance to a student who damaged school equipment in the Computer Laboratory and borrowed equipment such as computers, laptops, speakers, projector, and etc. While a student may also be required to comply with sanctions at Student Affairs and Service Office (SASO) if he/she commits a minor or a major offense before a clearance may be granted. In some situations, the Guidance Office may also be asked to fulfill counseling or other services before clearance is given. The said requirements above are necessary for requesting important documents to the registrar such as transcript of records (TOR), diploma or certificate of completion, and promotion to the next year level. 


As the verifications happen from different offices, the verification request and appointments can be time consuming for students and the staff. Students need to visit different offices to confirm their status and registrar offices have to wait for the manual verification before appointment and requisition requests can be made. As the verification is not in a single system that gives live updates of their clearance status, this can result in more days and struggling for the staff and students to know whether they fulfill the requirements needed in each office or not. These issues further highlight the need for a more efficient requisition request and appointment scheduling and clearance verification approach by the City College of Angeles Registrar’s office


Another problem with the current clearance verification process is the lack of validity and authenticity of the clearance forms signed by students submitted for checks. The fact that the clearance verification procedure is often executed offline with the help of paper-based clearance forms may lead to occasional doubt by registrar staff regarding the authenticity and authenticity of the signatures they process. Considering the existence of an opportunity for forgery and misrepresentation of such signatures in order to accomplish the clearance requirements, we need to focus on the stage of clearance verification. This becomes a problem with the registrar’s office regarding whether the students have cleared all the requirements. In this regard, the registrar’s office must verify the clearances to the concerned office, which will take more time and require more work and further delays on the requests and applications that will be affected by the registrar’s office. As such, there is a need for a more secure and more manageable process to automate and convert the status of the clearance approvals to avoid the possibility of fraud and processing of the requests with a more reliable validation and verification tool. 


Therefore this study aims to develop an Integrated Requisition and Scheduling System for City College of Angeles Registrar's Office, aligns with United Nations Sustainable Development Goals 4 (Quality Education). The system's goal with digital automation is to increase the accuracy, integrity, and accessibility of the scheduling management of the City College of Angeles Registrar’s Office.


Statement of the Problem
The current requisition and scheduling processes for City College of Angeles Registrar’s Office are manual and may produce inefficient outcomes. Students requiring academic records or trying to schedule services have experienced such challenges as difficulties navigating the process, long wait times, and little ability to track the status of the request in real-time.
A non-integrated platform makes it more complicated to handle requests for documents and making appointments, both for the user and for the administration. In the case of several platforms, it is frequent that requests are not seen, not well documented, and not easy to follow.
1. This study aims to identify the current processes used by the City College of Angeles Registrar’s Office in handling document requests, clearance verification, and registrar services scheduling.
2. This study seeks to discover the problems and challenges that are being encountered in the current manual process in terms of processing time, tracking of document requests, scheduling management, and accessibility of registrar services.
3. Can an Integrated Requisition and Scheduling System improve the process of submitting document requests, monitoring clearance status, and scheduling registrar transactions.
4. The study determines what are the important features and functionalities that are needed to be included in the system based on the requirements of the City College of Angeles registrar Office.
5. It is necessary to consider and evaluate the measures of the system’s efficiency based on its effectiveness, no-usable, operable performance, reliability, and accuracy. Usability and performance measures definition will allow us to analyze how the solution is effective for the problem.

Scope of the Study
This study focuses on the design and development of an Integrated Requisition and Scheduling System for the CCA Registrar’s Office. The system aims to digitalize the process of requesting academic documents, clearance verification, scheduling registrar related services, and generate a basic report which are currently handled and managed through manual process helping registrar officers efficiently manage students' transactions.


The proposed system allows students and authorized users to submit a request for academic documents, such as transcripts, certificates and other registrar related records. The system will provide automated scheduling features that assign specific available times and dates for the releasing of requested documents. The system will include clearance verification features, where registrar officers are able to view and monitor the clearance status of the students across various departments. Since registrar transactions require students to be cleared from different departments, the system will help monitor and verify the students clearance status before processing their requests. Additionally, the system also includes features such as request tracking to help registrar officers to effectively manage student transactions, providing a convenient and satisfactory experience for both students and staff. 


In addition, the systems should generate reports for the requisition of the Registrar’s Office for the admin to view the transaction, statistics on demand, and usage for administration purposes. The scope of the study is confined to the admin service of the CCA Registrar’s Office, and the system will be utilized by the registrar staff and students to requisition documents, upload files to the system, check their clearances, and book a schedule. It includes testing and assessment of the system or its effectiveness to evaluate if the proposed system can enhance the services of the registrar.


This study includes the analysis, system design, development, and evaluation from users of the proposed system to determine the effectiveness in improving the process, organization, and services provided by the City College of Angeles Registrar's Office.






Delimitations of the Study
This study is limited to only the development and implementation of an integrated requisition and scheduling system specifically for the City College of Angeles registrar office. The system will focus on digitizing the document request, scheduling appointments, viewing, and verification of the students clearance status from different departments.


The system will only support existing registrar service transactions, such as requests for scheduling and academic documents. While the system allows registrar personnel to view students' clearance, each department will still be responsible for the process of managing, approving and updating their own clearance records.


Additionally, the system is only limited in use within the City College of Angeles and will be accessed by the identified authorized users. The study also does not include the integration with other systems like enrollment systems, financial systems, grading systems, and external databases. Other aspects such as network expansion, future systems updates, new institutional policy implementation, and future long-term maintenance, are beyond the scope of this study.

METHODOLOGY
This chapter presents the research methods, procedures, development model, respondents, data gathering techniques, instruments, and evaluation processes used in creating the An Integrated Requisition and Scheduling System for CCA Registrar's Office. The methodology ensures that each stage of the study from planning to system evaluation is systematically executed to achieve the objectives outlined in the previous chapter.


Research Design
        The study utilized a Descriptive Research Design, a type of non-experimental research design that offers a factual, methodical, and precise depiction of current processes and the status quo of the system without altering any variables. As McCombes (2023) stated It is useful when not much is known yet about the topic or problem. Before you can research why something happens, you need to understand how, when and where it happens.
        The given design is suitable for the context to implement the An Integrated Requisition and Scheduling System for CCA Registrar's Office. This can be achieved through mapping, assessment, and presentation of  the current process of requesting and scheduling in the Registrar Office in City College of Angeles. 


System Development Methodology  
Figure 2. Agile Methodology
1. Plan - During this phase, high-level requirements were obtained through interviews and workflow observation of registrar current process, and scheduling. The purpose, system modules, schedule, and user roles were established. 
2. Design - System architecture, database schema, and UI models were created. Importantly, a user interface that encourages accurate data entry, quick record retrieval, and auditable access rights was developed. UI mock-ups were presented to stakeholders for initial feedback. Stakeholder feedback was used to make improvements to data fields, logging, and audit trails.
3. Develop - The implementation of the system was done through iterative sprints with the development of critical components by modules, such as the requisition module of the clearance, scheduling, and report generator. Using agile principles, adjustments were made based on the feedback received. 
4. Test - Testing was carried out during all stages of development. Unit testing and integration were conducted after each Sprint. Stakeholders from the Registrar Staffs provided input on functionality, security, and usability. Their inputs were incorporated continuously to improve record encoding, release documents, and auditing processes.
5. Deploy - After completing key tests, staging deployment was done to fully validate the system using the ISO 25010 software quality model. Stakeholder reviews were performed on the reliability, functionality, usability, and efficiency of the system. Performance and security tests were later carried out before deployment to production.
6. Review - Sprint reviews and retrospectives were conducted to assess development effectiveness and evaluate areas for improvement. Feedback from stakeholders was used to adapt future development iterations, improve features, and enhance the reliability and accuracy of the integrated requisition and scheduling document processing system.
7. Launch - After successfully completing the evaluation processes and getting the endorsement of the stakeholders, the system was rolled out for use. The development team ensured regular monitoring, maintenance of the system, and provision of necessary updates to keep the system running smoothly and guarantee precision in the requisition and scheduling for the Registrar Office.

        This module is designed to control access to the system ensuring that only authorized users such as student and registered personnel can access the assigned access privilege within the system and perform respective functions. Software Specifications
Operating System: Windows 10/11
Development Tools:
* Visual Studio Code: An Integrated Development Environment (IDE) supports almost every major programming language. Several ship in the box, like JavaScript, TypeScript, CSS, and HTML.
* Node.js (v18 or newer): JavaScript runtime used to execute the server-side application.
* npm: Node package manager used to install and manage project dependencies.
* Git / GitHub: Version control and collaborative repository hosting for the development team.

Frontend Frameworks:
* HTML5, CSS3, and vanilla JavaScript are used as the core frontend technologies for cross-browser compatibility and a lightweight presentation layer.
* EJS (Embedded JavaScript Templates): A server-side templating engine used to render role-based dashboards (student, registrar, administrator, and department officer) with dynamic data from the backend.
* Custom CSS (single-file stylesheet): Provides a modern and responsive user interface with a CCA-inspired blue color palette, role-based sidebars, status badges, and form components.

Backend Frameworks and Libraries:
* Express.js (v5): The primary backend web framework, handling routing, middleware, and HTTP request/response management.
* express-session: Session management library used to maintain authenticated user state across requests.
* bcryptjs: Password hashing library used to securely store user credentials.
* Multer: File-upload middleware used for accepting student-submitted payment proof images (JPG/PNG, max 5 MB).
* Tesseract.js: JavaScript-based Optical Character Recognition (OCR) engine used as the innovation feature to extract payment details (student name, Student ID, OR number, amount, and payment date) from uploaded receipts.
* Day.js: Lightweight date and time utility used for schedule formatting and slot generation.
* UUID: Library used to generate unique identifiers for users and records.

Database:
* SQLite 3: A lightweight, file-based relational database management system used to store users, document requests, department clearances, and system audit logs. SQLite was chosen for its portability, low configuration overhead, and suitability for a single-institution deployment such as the CCA Registrar's Office.

Web Browser (Client-Side):
* Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, or Mozilla Firefox (latest version): Any modern web browser that supports HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript is sufficient to access the web-based system.

System Architecture:
The system follows a client–server architecture deployed as a web-based application. The client (web browser) communicates with the Node.js/Express server through HTTP, which in turn reads from and writes to the SQLite database. Uploaded files are stored on the server's file system and served through a secured static route. The OCR module runs on the server side using Tesseract.js, processing uploaded payment proofs on demand when initiated by registrar staff.
